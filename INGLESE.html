INGLESE: THE SILICON VALLEY

The first civil electronics company was established in Silicon Valley and was called Hewlett-Packard in 1939. It was founded by two Stanford University graduates: Bill Hewlett and David Packard. The boom came in the 1950s, thanks to a technology incubator called Stanford Research Park created by Stanford University.

Silicon Valley is a nickname for the southern part of the San Francisco Bay Area in Northern California in the United States of America that serves as a global center for high tech, innovation and social media.
